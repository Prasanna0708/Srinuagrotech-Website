Here are some examples of different types of cereals:

  • Wheat: It is one of the world's most important crops. It contains protein and dietary fiber. In India, wheat is primarily used to make bread, chapatis, and other baked goods.
  • Rice: Rice is a staple food not only in India but all over the world. It is one of the most significant cereals on the list. It is a gluten-free meal that will keep you full for a long time.
  • Oats: Oat is a type of cereal that is commonly consumed in India as porridge. Steel cut, instant, and rolled oats are among the varieties available.
  • Barley: In India, one of the cereal names that is well-known is barley. Barley can be grown in the same conditions as wheat
  • Quinoa: Quinoa is an excellent source of protein if you are looking for a high-protein cereal. To meet the RDI for proteins, just one full bowl of quinoa will suffice.
  • Millet: Millets include a lot of different nutrients. Millets are rich in fiber, vitamins, minerals, and protein.

Cereals and pulses differ in that pulses lack the carbohydrates found in cereals, but they are high in other nutrients such as proteins and amino acids. Cereals are high in carbohydrates, but they are also high in dietary fiber, vitamins A, B6, B12, and C, calcium, phosphorus, amino acids, and other minerals. Pulses are dried seeds of legumes that come in a variety of shapes and sizes. This guide will provide you with a visual reference, description, and common names for some of the most common pulses:

  • Beans: Pulses include all canned or dried beans, peas, and lentils. They're great to include in your diet because they're high in protein and fiber, low in fat, and inexpensive to purchase.
  • Chickpeas: They are also referred to as chole. It is typically found in two varieties: smaller ones known as desi chana and larger ones known as Kabuli chana. For proper preparation, they are typically soaked overnight.
  • Lentils: This daal is extremely tasty. It is one of the most protein-rich lentils available on the market. This daal is also fed to racehorses due to its high nutritional value.

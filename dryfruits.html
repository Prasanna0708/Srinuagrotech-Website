Dried fruit is a fruit that has had almost all of its moisture removed by drying methods. During this process, the fruit shrinks, resulting in a small, energy-dense dried fruit. The most common are raisins, followed by dates, prunes, figs, and apricots.

Dried fruit is a fruit that has had the majority of its original water content removed, either naturally, by sun drying, or by using specialized dryers or dehydrators.

Spices and Condiments

Srinu Agrotech operates using a supply chain strategy for Spices and Condiments. A spice is a seed, fruit, root, bark, or other plant material that is primarily used to flavor or color food. Herbs, which are the leaves, flowers, or stems of plants used for flavoring or garnishing, are distinct from spices.

Spices not only enhance the flavor of food, but they are also high in vitamins B and C, iron, calcium, and other antioxidants. Spices dare derived from plant parts such as the bud, bark, root, flower, and fruit.

Many medical industries, including cosmetic, pharmaceutical, and aromatic perfumery, use spices.

Spice can be purchased in several forms, including fresh, whole-dried, and pre-ground dried. Spices are typically dried. Spices can be ground into a powder for ease of use. Because whole dried spices have the longest shelf life, they can be purchased and stored in larger quantities, making them less expensive per serving. Fresh spices, such as ginger, are typically more flavorful than dried spices, but they are more expensive and have a much shorter shelf life. Some spices, such as turmeric, are not always available fresh or whole, and must often be purchased ground. Small seeds, such as fennel and mustard seeds, are frequently used both whole and ground.

A condiment is a food preparation that is typically added to food after cooking to impart a specific flavor, enhance the flavor, or complement the dish. A table condiment, also known as a table sauce, is a condiment that is served separately from the food and is added to taste by the diner.

A condiment is a supplemental food (such as a sauce or powder) that is added to certain foods to impart a specific flavor, enhance their flavor, or, in some cultures, complement the dish, but cannot stand alone as a dish. Condiments not only improve the taste/flavor of a dish (for example, they frequently contain flavor-enhancing elements such as salt and umami) but they can also be used to add color/textural interest to whatever is being consumed. Spices were first written about in ancient Egyptian, Chinese, and Indian cultures. The Ebers Papyrus from early Egypt, dating from 1550 BCE, describes over 800 different medicinal remedies and procedures.
